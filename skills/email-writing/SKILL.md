# B2B Export Email Writing Guide

## Personalization Requirements

Every email must include:
- Recipient name and title.
- One specific product the company imports or distributes.
- When available: reference recent company news, trade show attendance, or a relevant regulatory change.

## Subject Line Formulas

Max length: **60 characters**.

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| [Their Company] + [value proposition] | "Acme Corp — FDA-certified ingredients at 20% below market" |
| Specific numbers | "Save 20% on certified [product] — 3 supplier options" |
| Question format | "Looking for a certified [product] supplier?" |

Never use: ALL CAPS, exclamation marks, or fake "RE:" prefixes.

## Email Body Structure

**Hook** (address a pain or need) → **Value** (your offer, specific benefit) → **CTA** (one clear next step).

## First Email Rules

- Maximum **3 sentences** in the body.
- Company name must appear in the subject line.
- CTA must be specific: request a meeting, offer samples, or share a catalog link.
- No attachments — link to catalog instead.

## Prohibited Patterns

- Generic salutations: "Dear Sir/Madam" or "To Whom It May Concern".
- Attachments in first email.
- Spam trigger words: "guaranteed", "act now", "limited time", "free", "click here".
- Exclamation marks in the subject line.

## Follow-up Email Rules

- Each follow-up must add new value: new information, a case study, or market data.
- Never write "just checking in" or "following up" without adding substance.
- Keep follow-ups shorter than the previous email.
- Reference the prior email briefly to maintain context.

## Tone

Use `settings.json` → `email.tone` (default: `professional`). Adapt tone per target market and buyer culture.
